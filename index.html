This is an absolute must-visit! Located in the Bay of Hiroshima, Miyajima Island offers:

  • Numerous beautiful temples to explore
  • Charming old township with authentic Japanese atmosphere
  • Abundant food options perfect for tourists
  • Option to hike or take cable car to the island's summit

Osaka ‚≠ê

An incredible city for food lovers and nightlife enthusiasts! Osaka has an amazing energy and endless options for entertainment.

Dontonbori Area (Recommended Stay)

Pro Tip: Stay in the Dontonbori area so you can easily walk around and explore!

This area is absolutely mind-blowing - it's essentially a suburb with countless tiny streets, and every single street is packed with small restaurants and bars. The sheer endlessness of options is incredible, and you'll be amazed by how many unique spots there are to discover.

Round1 Games Arcade

If you want to embrace your inner child, the Round1 games arcade in Osaka is unreal! A must-visit for gaming and entertainment enthusiasts.

Kyoto

Kyoto is definitely worth visiting, but be prepared for crowds - it's very busy. Consider doing a day trip from Osaka to avoid the hassle of changing accommodations.

Note: While you can find similar temples elsewhere with fewer tourists, Kyoto's unique advantage is the concentration of temples all located close to each other - which explains its massive popularity.

Nagoya & The Magome Pass

Consider stopping in Nagoya (accessible via Shinkansen) for a couple of nights to experience the historic Magome Pass.

Magome Pass Walk

This is an old trade route through the mountains connecting two beautifully preserved traditional villages. The experience includes:

  • A scenic mountain walk with stunning views
  • Two authentic old-style villages at each end
  • Interesting local culture and architecture
  • Excellent food options and unique trinkets

Tokyo

Where to Stay: Akasaka Area ‚≠ê

Recommended Location: The area between Akasaka-Mitsuke Station and Akasaka Station

After extensive research, this area stands out as the best spot to stay in Tokyo:

  • Excellent connectivity: Access to 4 metro lines
  • Central location: Easy to reach all major Tokyo attractions
  • Not overly busy: More peaceful than hyper-touristy areas
  • Great restaurants: Plenty of dining options within walking distance
  • Convenient: Take trains to explore during the day, easily find dinner near your accommodation at night

This area was absolutely awesome and made exploring Tokyo so much easier!

Important Tips

Hotel Size Warning ‚ö†Ô∏è

Many hotels in central Tokyo and Osaka are extremely small (space-efficient Japanese style).

For example, APA Hotels (a fairly large chain) often have rooms that are basically just a bed against one wall with minimal space to sit or move around. This is fine if you're out exploring all day, but not ideal if you want to relax in your room or are staying for an extended period.

Recommendation: Always check the actual room size and dimensions when booking your accommodation!
